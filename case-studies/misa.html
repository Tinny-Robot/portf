MISA — AI bookkeeping for African businesses. Just talk.

MISA records sales, tracks inventory, manages debtors, and delivers financial advice through voice or chat — in English, Pidgin, Hausa, Yoruba, or Igbo. Built for market traders, shops, pharmacies, restaurants, and growing SMEs who need bookkeeping without complex accounting software.

Brand & product identity

Problem

Most African businesses still run on notebooks, memory, and informal cash records. Traditional accounting apps are complex, English-only, and data-entry heavy — so owners abandon them within months.

  • Cash-heavy transactions with no reliable digital trail.
  • Manual bookkeeping that does not scale with daily trade volume.
  • Poor financial visibility leading to restocking mistakes, debtor losses, and cashflow stress.
  • Language barriers excluding Hausa, Igbo, Yoruba, and Pidgin-speaking business owners.

Solution

MISA turns everyday business conversation into structured financial records and actionable intelligence. No forms, no menus — owners speak or type naturally and MISA handles categorization, storage, reporting, and advice.

  • Voice-first and chat-first transaction capture.
  • Conversational onboarding that adapts fields to each business niche.
  • Inventory, debtors, invoices, and financial reports on demand.
  • AI business advisor with personalized recommendations over time.

How MISA works

  1. Tell MISA what happened — e.g. “Sold rice worth ₦15,000” or “Bought shoes for ₦80,000”.
  2. MISA records and categorizes it — transactions are tagged, sorted, and stored securely in the cloud.
  3. Ask questions anytime — “How much did I make this week?”, “Who owes me money?”, “Which product sold most last month?”
  4. Get actionable insights — cost-cut suggestions, restocking alerts, debtor reminders, and trend analysis.

Conversational product design

Onboarding is handled entirely by MISA in chat — capturing business profile, communication tone, and niche-specific fields before the user records their first transaction.

  • Dynamic schema per business type (e.g. inventory fields for pharmacies, ingredient costs for restaurants).
  • Transaction confirmation — MISA validates amount, category, and date before saving.
  • Agentic tooling — MISA queries internal analytics and transaction history to answer financial questions.
  • Credit-based usage model (MISA Credits) with prepaid tiers for Starter, Growth, and Enterprise.
